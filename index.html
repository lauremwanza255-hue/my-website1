Our Story

Home / About Us
The Saffira mill, est. 1998
Since 1998

A spice house built on one stubborn belief

Saffira began at a single market stall in the old quarter, where our founder Anaya Rao refused to sell anything she hadn't smelled, crushed and tasted herself. Three decades later, that stubbornness is still the whole business model.

We buy whole spices — never pre-ground — directly from the farmers who grow them. Tellicherry peppercorns from the Malabar coast. Cardamom from the misty Idukki hills. Cloves from Pemba island. Each lot is cleaned, graded, gently roasted and milled to order, so the jar that reaches you is alive with oils that supermarket spice lost months ago.

No fillers. No anti-caking agents. No spice older than its last harvest. Just the real, fragrant thing — the way your grandmother's kitchen smelled.

Sourced Direct

We skip the auction houses and buy whole spices straight from growers, paying above fair-trade rates for top grades and lasting relationships.

02

Spices are roasted and ground in small batches the week they ship — because a ground spice starts dying the moment it's cut.
